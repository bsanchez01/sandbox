🔍 Leadership Intelligence Briefing — Fall 2025
Executive Signals
📌
AALA remains the largest program at 28.1% (320 students) — a significant concentration risk. Liberal Arts, Elementary Education, and Business Studies together account for 86% of total enrollment.
🔢
Developmental Math exposure is critically high at 68.0% — nearly 2 in 3 students require developmental math support. This has direct implications for resource allocation, faculty capacity, and time-to-degree.
⚠️
Semester credit completion rate is 67.3% — students are earning only about two-thirds of credits they enroll in. This is a key efficiency and financial aid risk indicator requiring targeted intervention.
🌟
First-Time students jumped to 24.0% (273), a significant increase from the prior snapshot (9.3%). This signals a stronger new-student pipeline and improved recruitment — a positive strategic development.
🏫
Uliga Campus accounts for 93.4% of all enrollment (1,063 students). Satellite campuses — Jaluit (34), Wotje (25), Ebeye (8), and Arrak (6) — serve critical outer-island populations with limited access.
🎯
BAEE leads academic performance with a 3.05 avg semester GPA and 83.3% credit completion — the strongest outcomes among major programs. CCCHOW and DUAL also perform above 3.5 GPA but at small scale.
📉
907 students (79.7%) ended the term Continuing — the strongest retention signal across all versions of this report. Only 22 Inactive students remain, with 95 still "To Be Determined" pending grade resolution.
👩‍🎓
Female enrollment leads at 55.4% (630 vs. 508 male). Average student age is 23.1 years; 43.7% of students are age 18–20, confirming a predominantly traditional-age population with a meaningful adult learner segment.

Risk & Opportunity Flags
System-generated alerts based on enrollment thresholds and performance benchmarks
⚠️ Risk Indicators
🔴
Dev Math at 68.0% is an institutional crisis signal. More than 2 in 3 students require developmental math. This drives extended time-to-degree, higher costs, and elevated attrition risk.
🔴
Semester completion rate of 67.3% is critically low. Students enrolled in 14,199 credits but earned only 9,553. One-third of enrolled credits are not completed — a direct financial aid and satisfactory progress risk.
🔴
AALA holds 28.1% of all enrollment. A single program concentration of this magnitude exposes the institution to significant headcount risk if faculty, funding, or student demand shifts.
🟡
84 Inactive students at term end need immediate outreach. Combined with 364 unresolved "To Be Determined" standings, 39.4% of students have ambiguous or negative end-of-term status.
🟡
20 withdrawals this term (1.8%). While low in absolute number, tracking withdrawal timing and program distribution can help predict and prevent future drops.
🟡
Outer-island campuses have very low full-time rates. Wotje at 20.0% and Jaluit at 32.4% suggest part-time enrollment dominates satellite sites — impacting FTE, financial aid, and completion metrics.
✅ Opportunity Indicators
🟢
First-Time enrollment more than doubled to 273 (24.0%). This is a strong indicator of improved recruitment pipeline and new student interest — an area to sustain and build on strategically.
🟢
BAEE leads all major programs with a 3.05 avg semester GPA and 83.3% credit completion rate. The Bachelor of Education program is a model worth studying and replicating.
🟢
CCCHOW, DUAL, CCT all show GPA above 3.4. Certificate and continuing education programs attract highly motivated learners — a segment worth expanding for workforce development and adult education.
🔵
Nursing (ASNURS) shows strong academic performance with 2.63 avg semester GPA and 70.2% completion rate — above the institutional average. This program supports a critical national workforce need.
🔵
240 students (25.0%) hold a 3.0+ cumulative GPA. This cohort is a natural pool for academic honors recognition, peer mentoring programs, and scholarship incentives to drive retention.
🔵
Enrollment surged in July–August with 673 registrations in August alone. Early outreach starting in June could convert more early registrations and reduce last-minute enrollment stress on administration.

Analytical Assumptions & Governance Notes

  • Source: "Official Enrollment Report" worksheet from Official_Enrollment_Report2.xlsx — Fall 2025, AY 2025–26, Semester 1.
  • 4 duplicate SONIS_IDs detected and removed (AL1826739, JA9159920, KO8394582, RA0010708). Dashboard is based on 1,138 unique students. Withdrawn count of 20 is from the raw file before deduplication.
  • FTE calculated as: Full-Time (707) + Part-Time (431) × 0.33 = 849, per institutional formula.
  • GPA averages exclude 0.00 values to avoid skewing performance metrics. Zero-GPA students are counted separately.
  • Semester completion rate = SEM_CREDITS_EARNED / CREDITS_ENROLLED = 9,553 / 14,199 = 67.3%.
  • Cumulative completion ratio = CUM_CREDITS_EARNED / CUM_CREDITS_ATTEMPTED = 44,243 / 39,459 = 112.1%. Values above 100% reflect transfer credit entries or data methodology in the SIS.
  • Developmental education flags (HAS_DEVED_ENGLISH, HAS_DEVED_MATH) sourced directly from student-level fields in this dataset.
  • Age calculated as of September 1, 2025 (Fall 2025 term start). 90 records had missing or unparseable BIRTHDATE — excluded from age analysis.
  • No personally identifiable information (names, SSN, email, SIS_ID) is displayed anywhere in this dashboard.
